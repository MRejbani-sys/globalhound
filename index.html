This Week's Intelligence Drop

NEW

🇩🇪 Germany — Opportunity Card Expansion

Germany's Federal Employment Agency has announced an expansion of the Chancenkarte (Opportunity Card) program for 2025. The updated criteria now include professionals with a minimum of 3 years of work experience (down from 5) and degrees from 180 additional countries. The August 1st application window opens new pathways for professionals from Africa, South Asia, and Southeast Asia who were previously excluded. Key requirements: B1 German language OR 3 years experience in a shortage occupation. Full report in your Country Hub.

🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia — Vision 2030 Phase 3 Hiring Surge

NEOM, Saudi Aramco, and the Public Investment Fund have collectively signalled 1,200+ new expatriate roles across Q2 2025. Focus areas: civil engineering, project management, digital transformation, and specialist healthcare. Packages remain highly competitive with tax-free salaries, housing, and annual flight allowances. Check this week's GCC Private Market for 6 new listings from this surge.

🇸🇬 Singapore — Tech.Pass Criteria Update

Singapore's Ministry of Manpower has lowered the Tech.Pass fixed monthly salary threshold from SGD 22,500 to SGD 18,000 effective April 1st, 2025. This change expands eligibility to thousands more mid-senior tech professionals. Qualifying criteria: Either earn SGD 18K/mo from a leading tech firm, OR have 5+ years of experience and hold equity/leadership in a tech startup valued at $500M+.

Why GlobalHound Exists

We built what we wished existed.

International job searching is broken. Talented professionals spend months applying to employers who don't sponsor visas, researching countries with outdated data, and missing out on opportunities that are never publicly listed — especially in the GCC region where the best roles are shared exclusively through private networks.

GlobalHound was built to close that gap. We aggregate, verify, and curate the intelligence that globally mobile professionals need: which countries are genuinely welcoming to international talent, which employers actually deliver on their relocation promises, and what the real packages look like in markets like Saudi Arabia, UAE, and Qatar.
